# Productization Mode

AOI distinguishes research claim ceilings from product-development permission.

## Research Mode

Research Mode is used when reading internal research sources, design notes, or theoretical source sets. In this mode, AOI should preserve claim ceilings:

- internal source status does not prove product readiness;
- internal field validation does not prove external field validation;
- internal security reasoning does not prove security certification;
- internal legal reasoning does not prove legal readiness;
- internal theorem language does not settle external theorems or market claims.

Research Mode prevents unsupported public claims. It does not prohibit implementation.

## Productization Mode

Productization Mode is used when building software artifacts from research-inspired ideas.

In this mode, internal research may be used to design and implement:

- schemas;
- Python models;
- ranking algorithms;
- MCP/API prototypes;
- benchmark reports;
- fixture pipelines;
- GitHub-ready repositories;
- tests and eval harnesses.

The core rule is simple: internal research source status does not automatically prove product readiness, but internal research can be used to build products. Productization is allowed. Product claims require product evidence.

## Commercialization Mode

Commercialization Mode is used for experiments around packaging, demos, distribution, pricing research, ecosystem testing, or market feedback.

Commercialization experiments are allowed when they preserve AOI's claim boundaries. They still require product evidence for public product-readiness, security, legal, compliance, procurement, or purchasing claims.

## Public Claim Mode

Public Claim Mode is used when writing README copy, launch posts, docs, marketplace descriptions, demo pages, or customer-facing statements.

In this mode, claims must be backed by product evidence, not merely internal research intent. AOI may be described as experimental read-only software, benchmark tooling, and source-traced objective comparison infrastructure. AOI must not be described as an official standard, quality guarantee, legal/security/compliance certification, purchasing advice, or action-execution system.
